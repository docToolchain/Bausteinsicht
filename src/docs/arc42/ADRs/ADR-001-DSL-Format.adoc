= ADR-001: Choice of DSL Format
:doctype: article

* *Status*: Accepted
* *Date*: reverse-engineered (2026-Q2)
* *Source*: OQ-5, OQ-6 team answers; reverse-engineered from
`internal/model/loader.go:155-227`, `templates/sample-model.jsonc`,
QUESTION_TREE.adoc Q-3.9.2 / Q-3.9.3.

== Context

Bausteinsicht needs a textual representation of architecture models
that can be:

* edited by hand and by LLM agents,
* parsed cheaply with little custom tooling,
* synchronised bidirectionally with draw.io,
* validated and auto-completed by IDEs without plugins.

Six candidates were evaluated _(OQ-5 team answer)_:

. *JSON + JSONC + Schema* ŌĆö JSON with comments and trailing commas, IDE
  support via JSON Schema.
. *TypeScript* ŌĆö express the model as TypeScript code.
. *Custom DSL* ŌĆö design a textual DSL parser of our own.
. *Structurizr DSL* ŌĆö adopt the existing Structurizr DSL.
. *LikeC4 DSL* ŌĆö adopt the LikeC4 DSL.
. *PlantUML C4* ŌĆö model lives in PlantUML.

== Decision

*JSONC + JSON Schema* (option 1).

== Rationale

_(OQ-5 team answer)_

. *No parser to write.* `encoding/json` plus a small JSONC stripper
  _(`internal/model/loader.go:155-227`)_ is all the parsing the
  product needs.
. *Bidirectional sync is trivial with JSON.* Every value has a stable
  path; comment-preserving patches operate on raw bytes
  _(`internal/model/patch.go`)_.
. *Universal IDE support via JSON Schema.* `templates/sample-model.jsonc:2`
  pins `$schema`; VS Code, JetBrains, and Neovim all auto-complete and
  validate without a plugin.
. *LLM-native.* LLMs generate JSON more reliably than any other format.
. *Comments via JSONC.* Mitigates JSON's main complaint.

The trade-off (lower compactness than a custom DSL) is outweighed by
tooling, sync, and LLM integration advantages.

== Pugh matrix (weighted, -1/0/+1)

[cols="2,1,^1,^1,^1,^1,^1,^1", options="header"]
|===
| Criterion | Weight | JSONC | TypeScript | Custom DSL | Structurizr | LikeC4 | PlantUML

| Sync round-trip simplicity        | 5 | +1 |  0 | -1 | -1 | -1 | -1
| LLM-friendliness (write reliably) | 4 | +1 |  0 |  0 |  0 |  0 | -1
| IDE support without plugins       | 4 | +1 | +1 | -1 |  0 |  0 |  0
| No parser to maintain             | 3 | +1 | -1 | -1 | +1 | +1 | +1
| Compactness / readability         | 2 |  0 |  0 | +1 | +1 | +1 | +1
| Comments + version-control fit    | 2 | +1 | +1 |  0 | +1 | +1 |  0
| Tooling / linter ecosystem        | 1 | +1 | +1 | -1 |  0 |  0 |  0

| *Total (weighted)*                |   | *+20* | *+5* | *-9* | *+5* | *+5* | *-3*
|===

JSONC scored +20 (highest) ŌĆö confirmed by OQ-5 team answer.

== Consequences

* The team writes and maintains a small JSONC stripper instead of an
  external parser dependency.
* The schema is the single source of validation truth and must be kept
  in sync with the Go `Element` struct
  _(see link:../chapters/11_risks.adoc[Risk-D-3])_.
* Bidirectional sync is implemented as a three-way diff (no CRDTs).
